What Is a Workout Split?

A workout split is how you divide your training across the week. It helps you balance muscle groups and leave time for recovery.

Choosing a Split

  • Be honest about how many days you can train.
  • Plan at least one rest or light activity day.
  • Use basic movements like squats, presses, and rows.

3-Day Full Body Split

Good for anyone with a busy schedule.

NOTE: *You should rest at least once in between days*

Day Focus Example Exercises
Day 1 Full Body A Squats, Bench Press, Lat Pulldowns, Shoulder Press, Leg Curls, Bicep Curls, Face Pulls
Day 2 Rest Focus Recovery and Proper Nutrition
Day 3 Full Body B Romainian Deadlift, Seated Rows, Incline Press, Split Squats, Lat Raises, Tricep Pushdown, Calf Raises
Day 4 Rest Focus Recovery and Proper Nutrition
Day 5 Full Body *Repeat A for 3rd workout, For next week do B twice and alternate* (A-B-A and B-A-B)
Day 6 Rest Focus Recovery and Proper Nutrition

5 Day PPL (Push, Pull, Legs)

This split has a bit more volume but still allows recovery time

NOTE: *You can choose which 2 days to rest*

3 Sets of 10 or 1 Warm Up and 2 sets to Failure

Day Focus Example Exercises
Day 1 Push Variations of: Bench Press + Incline press, Tricep Push down + Overhead Extension, Lateral raises
Day 2 Pull Variations of: Preacher + Incline curl, Lat pulldowns, Rows, Rear Delts, Reverse Forearm Curls
Day 3 Legs Variations of: Leg Press, Calf Raises, Hamstring Curl, Leg Extensions, Abductors
Day 4 Rest Focus Recovery and Proper Nutrition
Day 5 Push Variations of: Bench Press + Incline Press, Tricep Push down + Overhead Extension, Lateral raise
Day 6 Pull Variations of: Preacher + Incline curl, Lat pulldowns, Rows, Rear Delts, Reverse Forearm Curls
Day 7 Rest Focus Recovery and Proper Nutrition
